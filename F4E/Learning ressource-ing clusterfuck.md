## Wavedashing

A Wave Dash is a physics engine exploit achieved by executing a short hop immediately followed by a dash. 

It manipulates the game's movement calculations, forcing arcing traversal rather than a standard straight dash. It grants massive speeds and range, making it crucial for competitive mobility.

Arcs are a very important principle in animation. Wave dashes should be an integral part of the game, easy and comfortable to perform with animations specifically made for the mechanic.

## Tecking

Tecking walls and surfaces in Platform fighters is the act of using the environment for the players advantage

When a character interacts with a surface (a wall, ceiling, floor, or corner), the game registers a specific collision state. A skilled player can use this interaction to control *how* a character's velocity and trajectory change upon impact.

**Tecking involves:**

**Wall Jumps/Wall Kicks:** Instead of running into a wall, the player initiates an impulse force away from it by jumping or attacking while touching the surface. This allows rapid propulsion upward (Wall Jump) or sideways (Wall Kick). For example, launching diagonally off a vertical wall bypasses opponents positioned directly in front.

**Edge Guarding/Surface Trapping:** The player forces the opponent into a specific position relative to a surface—often at an edge or corner—and then dictates their fate by moving along that surface. By outpacing the trapped opponent (e.g., running along a ceiling), the attacker can initiate a ledge grab or force them into a disadvantageous spot.

Every interaction serves a strategic purpose:

When hit or knocked back, a player can "teck" themselves into a corner or against a wall to stabilize their trajectory, preventing them from being immediately pushed off the stage (a process known as *recovery*). They use the surface's resistance to arrest unwanted movement.

## Wavelanding

Wavelanding is similar to/the same as wavedashing with one key difference

The idea of wavelanding is to wavedash into the ground in the fastest way for an easy and quick follow up attack on the ground.
